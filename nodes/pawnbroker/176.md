Inside a long glass-fronted counter stretches almost the full length of the room displaying rings, gemstones, watches, ornaments and other valuable curios beneath the glow of hanging brass lamps.

The walls are lined floor to ceiling with huge square shelves packed tightly with objects of every imaginable sort. Armour, books, lanterns, statues, musical instruments and strange mechanical devices fill every available space.

Upon the counter sits an elaborate board game midway through a match. Tiny painted wizards, dragons and knights stand frozen across the carved wooden board as though whomever is playing is mid-game.

Standing behind the counter is an elderly man dressed in a dark green suit with round spectacles perched low upon his nose. He is just concluding business with a young blonde woman wearing a pale orange dress.

‘I don’t know where you kids find these things,’ the pawnbroker says paying her for the long pointed crown she is selling.

The woman shrugs, giving you a cheerful smile as she thanks the pawnbroker and heads out into the street. The pawnbroker then turns his attention towards you.

'And how can I help you, my good man?'
