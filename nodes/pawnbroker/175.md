You enter a wealthier part of town where the streets grow wider and the buildings taller. Imposing rows of maroon brick buildings line the street, their ground floors dominated by large polished display windows, while long narrow windows rise above in perfectly ordered rows.

Your attention is drawn to a pawnbroker’s shop. Hanging above the entrance are three copper balls, green with age, suspended beneath a sign running the full length of the building which simply reads LOANS.

The wide display window is crowded with a myriad of objects: silver goblets, regal paintings, jewellery, weapons, clocks, masks, strange mechanical devices and other treasures whose origins are impossible to guess.
