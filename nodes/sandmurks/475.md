The wolf, you now recognise as Aragh, pads over and studies you with his bright yellow eyes. When he is sure you are OK, he says, "{playerStats.wizardName}, you old fool, what are you doing bedding down with sandmurks?"

"Thought when I gave you that whistle we might share a steak sometime. Never thought I'd be cracking sandmurk necks," he chuckles. "Felt good though, taking out some of Ommadon's filthy brew." He wrinkles his grey snout and snorts. "They do taste foul though."

When your head stops ringing, you thank the wolf.

"Arr," he says dismissively, "you saved my life once. It would be rude not to return the favour."

You chat with the wolf well into the night. When you finally turn in, he chooses to remain and keep watch, making sure there are no surprises while you sleep. You sleep well that night under the watchful eye of Aragh.

By morning your strength has largely returned. After thanking Aragh once more, you pack your belongings and prepare to leave.
