You open the door onto a plainly furnished chamber lit by a brightly burning white flame in a wide shallow dish sitting in a high up alcove.

A large table dominates the room, scattered with ledgers, marked diagrams and a half-eaten meal. A heavy chair is pulled back from it.

Standing beside the table is a slim yellow-skinned woman with jet-black eyes. Her dark livery is stretched across her slender shoulders, her sleeves rolled back to reveal forearms sleaved with floral tattoos, spiralling out from a central mechanised skull motifs. She is cleaning something off a huge cudgel, which she holds deftly in one hand.

She looks up at you and sighs, then leans back against the desk, the cudgel still in her hand.

"That door," she says evenly, "should have stayed closed."
