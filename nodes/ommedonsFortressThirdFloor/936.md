The room is not what you expected. There is no evidence of war machines. Some half-assembled devices, too small to be of use in battle, are scattered about, but mostly the room looks like an office. One which the workers have, as a unit, downed tools and abandoned.

The room is in a state somewhere between studious industry and disarray. Books are scattered across desks as if having just been consulted. Diagrams are pinned to boards, parchments are rolled out on benches and held flat with small stones and ink bottles. Chairs are pushed back from tables as if the occupants meant to come straight back to them.

A large communal table dominates the centre of the room. Upon it rests what looks like an enormous brass many-pointed compass. It ticks softly, its needle twitching whenever you move.

There is a button on either side of the compass, each engraved with bold clear wording.

The first button reads:
"DO IT NOW!"

The other reads:
"DO IT LATER"
