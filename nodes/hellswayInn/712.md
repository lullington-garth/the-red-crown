The old man glances up as you approach and gestures towards an empty chair.

"Seems my companion is late. Why not join me?"

His face is lined with age and hard labour. He raises his ale to you.

"Crimshaw Shackleback," he says. "Once a miner. Now... well, we'll see when my companion arrives."

You talk for a while, Crimshaw telling stories about his days in the mines.

"Used to be a village not far from here, before the Keep went up and that monster they call the Ogre of Gormley Keep arrived. Size of a dragon, that thing, and twice as mean."

He shakes his head.

"We had to abandon the mine, of course." He pokes morosely at the fire. "No doubt buried beneath all that cursed red sand by now. It was nearly dry anyway. Nothing left down there but beasts, flowers, and some religious nuts that worship them. The flowers, I mean. Something to do with sleeping dragons, I think."

The flowers sound intriguing and you would have liked to ask more about them, but just then Crimshaw's companion arrives.

You thank the aged miner and give him and his guest some privacy.
