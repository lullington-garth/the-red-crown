You wait until you are sure that the dragons have seen you. You let them swoop on droves, waiting until you can see the gleam of their scales crimson, bronze, black and silver in the storm light. You wait until you can see the unnatural fury imposed by Ommadon burning in their golden eyes.

Then you raise the Flute of Healing Sleep to your lips and begin to play. You choose a simple, slow melody, one you know well, one you know you can play without thought.

The effects are immediate. The lilting melody, so simple in form, hits the dragons like a shock wave. Those closest to you have just enough time for their eyes to clear of Ommadon's poisonous hold, show a moment of shocked confusion before sleep consumes them.

Their wings slow, their heads droop and they fall from the sky. They do not fall like comets. There is a deep magic in dragons. They fall like leaves shaken from an autumnal tree, drifting gently to the ground.

The dragons' exceptional hearing ensures that even those on the very edges of the horde are caught in the peaceful healing magic of the flute.  

You play until the ground is littered with dragons for as far as the eye can see.
