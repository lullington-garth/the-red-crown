Some distance from the cottage, the wolf slows and looks up at you with large golden eyes. To your astonishment it speaks in a calm, deep voice.

‘You have courage, mage, and few among men would risk themselves for one such as I.’ The wolf bows its great head. ‘I am Aragh.’

Around its neck hangs a bone whistle tied with a leather cord. Aragh bites through the cord and nudges the whistle towards you.

‘Take this,’ he says ‘Should you ever need my aid, blow hard upon the whistle and I shall hear your call, wherever I may roam I will come.’

You thank the wolf, placing the whistle safely into your backpack. Aragh gives a final nod before disappearing silently into the dark woods.
