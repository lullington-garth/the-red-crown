The wolf isn’t as badly hurt as it might have been. It looks up at you with large golden eyes and to your astonishment, it thanks you.

‘You have courage, mage,’ it says, ‘I’m surprised that you did not recognise me as a magical creature. They did,’ He nods in the direction the men fled. The wolf bows its great head. ‘I am Aragh. And I owe you a great debt. There is a whistle around my neck. Take it. Should you ever need my aid, blow hard upon the whistle and I shall hear your call, wherever I may roam I will come.’

You thank the wolf, untie the whistle and place it safely into your backpack. Aragh gives a final nod then disappears silently into the dark woods.
