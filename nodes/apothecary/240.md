You head into The Fitch which is quiet for this time of day. The first shop you encounter is an apothecary whose darkened windows and bolted door suggest that they are not open for business today. A brightly painted sign hangs crooked above the entrance, heralding Capstow Paddock’s All Stop Apothecary. The display behind the polished bay glass shows a myriad of bottles, jars and curious instruments glinting, puffing and spinning in the shopkeepers attempt to grab passersby’s attention. You are just thinking that it’s a shame that it’s closed when you notice that one of the rear shutters has been left unsecured, swaying slightly in the cold afternoon breeze and offering a possible way inside. The narrow street is quiet. You could slip in without being seen.

Will you…
