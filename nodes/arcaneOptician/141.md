The strange assortment of objects in the window bears little resemblance to the shop within. Stepping through the door, you find yourself in what appears to be a lens crafter’s workshop, though unlike any ordinary optician you have ever seen.

The air smells faintly of lamp oil, brass polish and heated glass. Shelves line every wall from floor to ceiling, crowded with polished lenses, coloured spectacles, crystal monocles and delicate brass instruments covered in tiny adjustment screws. Some lenses shimmer with pale inner light, while others distort the room in unsettling ways as you glance through them. Half-finished devices lie scattered across crowded workbenches beside jeweller’s tools, wire frames and bubbling alchemical solutions. Suspended from the ceiling is an elaborate arrangement of magnifying glasses that slowly rotate of their own accord.

Behind one bench sits a small man with a thick iron-grey beard. One eye is hidden behind a curious multi-layered eyepiece of brass and crystal as he peers intently at a glowing lens held in a clamp. After a moment he grunts with satisfaction, sets the lens carefully aside and wipes his hands upon a stained leather apron before looking up at you.

“Handle Loveacre,” he says with a slight bow of the head. “Specialist Arcane Optician. Purveyor of magical glass and enchanted lenses. What can I help you with?”

Without waiting for an answer, he reaches beneath the counter and produces a slim silver monocle resting upon a velvet cloth.

“Now this beauty came in only this morning — the Monocle of Appraisal. Lovely little piece. Reveals the magical nature of any item you study through it. Can’t spot curses, mind you, but if it could, you almost certainly couldn’t afford it.” He chuckles to himself. “Fifteen Gold Pieces if you’re interested.”

“I’ve other curiosities besides,” He gestures proudly to the countless glittering lenses and spectacles around the shop. “Seeing lenses. Reading lenses. Hunting lenses. One or two I probably shouldn’t sell at all… lenses.” He chuckles again. “So then… what do you fancy?”
