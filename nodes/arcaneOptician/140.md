You continue along Crow Arch Lane. The crooked street bends between leaning houses whose upper floors almost touch overhead, shutting out much of the afternoon daylight.
Before long you come across an unusual-looking shop set slightly back from its neighbours behind a scrolled iron railing. Its windows are cluttered with strange objects half-hidden beneath dust-covered cloths: the outline of a birdcage, the glimmer of coloured glass, the curve of something that might be a horn or antler. No sign hangs above the door and from the street it is impossible to tell what goods are sold within. Yet something about the place feels oddly inviting, as though it is waiting for curious travellers to step inside.
