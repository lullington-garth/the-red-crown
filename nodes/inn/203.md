“This man is not a bank,” you say to the knight. This gets some shouts of approval from the crowd. “Perhaps if you do not have the coin, you could pawn some of your armour. That would be the honourable thing to do.”

At first the knight looks appalled, then he lowers his head and says, “Quite right, old chap, I will do that.” He turns to the smithy. “Come my good man, let us get you the coin I owe, and hear no more of this dispute.” He leaves with the smithy in the direction of the pawnbrokers. 

The crowd soon thins with the action now over. You are just turning to leave when a short bald man hurries up to you with a wrapped bundle.
