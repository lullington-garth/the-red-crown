Brookmeadow Lane comes to an end beside the river, where a sturdy wooden bridge spans the water before continuing into Pilgrims Way. Beyond the crossing, the outskirts of Brookdown Crossing give way once more to scattered cottages and quiet lanes.

As you approach the bridge, a weathered wooden panel lying beside the road suddenly shifts. What you had taken for a discarded pallet slides neatly aside, and a dirty-faced girl pokes her head through the opening beneath. Scrambling up to sit on the edge of the hole, she flashes a mischievous grin.

"Looking to get somewhere in town?" she asks. "There's tunnels down there what go everywhere worth going. I know 'em better than anyone. One Gold Piece and I'll have you there before you can walk it. Where do you want to go. I can take you to Market Street, Clock Tower Street, even The Fitch. There tunnels to all of 'em down there."

She waits expectantly, one hand held out for your coin.
