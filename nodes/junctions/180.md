Silk Street comes to an end at the river, where an elegant stone bridge crosses the broad waters into the district known as The Fitch. Well-dressed merchants and prosperous townsfolk make frequent use of the crossing, their footsteps mingling with the steady rumble of carts bound for the markets beyond.

Nestled beside the bridge stands a brightly painted wagon draped with embroidered banners and strings of coloured lanterns. Before it, a robed wizard sits comfortably beneath a striped awning, watching the passing crowds with an expression of quiet amusement. A neatly lettered sign proclaims that, for the modest fee of 1 Gold Piece, he will transport travellers instantly to a choice of destinations anywhere within Brookdown Crossing. Today he is offering: Market Street, Clock Tower Street or, if you prefer somewhere a little quieter, Pilgrims Way.
