Crow Arch Lane comes to an end at the banks of the broad river that winds through Brookdown Crossing. A sturdy stone bridge arches gracefully over the water, carrying the road onwards into the busy thoroughfare of Market Street, where the sounds of commerce drift across on the breeze.

Moored beside the bridge, a weathered ferry rocks gently against a timber landing. Its grey-bearded ferryman leans upon a long pole, watching the river with the patience of a man who has spent a lifetime upon it. Catching your eye, he offers to carry you downstream to any of several landings for the modest sum of 1 Gold Piece. He says that the landing are open at Clock Tower Street, The Fitch and Pilgrims Way on the outskirst of town.
