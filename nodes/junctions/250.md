The Fitch grows steadily busier as you make your way through its winding streets. The district has a well-lived-in feel, with closely packed houses, independent shops and the constant ebb and flow of townsfolk going about their business. Somewhere ahead, you can hear the distant swell of laughter and conversation, and beyond the rooftops you catch sight of a lively tavern standing farther down the street, though it is still too distant to make out its sign or finer details.

To the west, a narrow lane leads into Tocaboo Copse, a small stand of mature trees that marks the edge of Brookdown Crossing. Dappled sunlight filters through the branches onto a well-worn footpath, which winds quietly towards the outskirts of the town and the open countryside beyond.
