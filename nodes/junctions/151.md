You hand the ferryman a single Gold Piece and step carefully aboard. With a practised shove of his pole, the little ferry glides away from the landing and slips into the gentle current.

The bustle of Brookdown Crossing takes on a different character from the water. Stone bridges span the river overhead, warehouses and riverside workshops line the banks, and the cries of merchants mingle with the calls of waterbirds wheeling above the slow-moving current. 

Before long, the ferryman guides the boat alongside the landing at Clock Tower Street.
