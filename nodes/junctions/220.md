The buildings begin to thin as you reach the next junction, where the busy streets of Brookdown Crossing gradually give way to quieter surroundings. The sounds of traders and craftsmen are more distant here, carried only faintly on the breeze.

Ahead, St Cinders Path continues south, following a peaceful route towards the edge of town. Flanked by old hedgerows and scattered trees, the well-worn path seems little used beyond the occasional traveller seeking a quieter way into the countryside.

To the west, a broader road known as The Sidings leads back towards the town. Though modest at first, you can see it opens onto a much livelier street beyond, where the bustle of merchants, pedestrians and passing carts fills the air once more.
