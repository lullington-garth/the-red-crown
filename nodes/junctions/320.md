With the tavern well behind you, you arrive at a broad three-way junction where the character of the district begins to change.

Straight ahead, the road continues south as The Old Curiosity. The buildings become older and more eccentric in appearance, their leaning upper storeys crowding over the cobbles as though guarding the secrets of generations past.

To the east, The Quicksilver Bang leads between rows of tightly packed buildings towards a noticeably livelier part of the town. The murmur of busy streets drifts towards you, accompanied by the occasional shout of a trader and the rumble of passing carts.

The third path follows the banks of the narrow stream known as November Pass, winding away towards the outskirts of Brookdown Crossing. Shaded by willows and alder trees, the peaceful route offers a welcome respite from the noise of the town.
