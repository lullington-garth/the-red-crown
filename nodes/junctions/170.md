Bellfounders Way ends at the river, where a broad stone bridge spans the water before continuing into the busy streets of Clock Tower Street. Traders, labourers and laden carts stream steadily across, making the crossing one of the busiest in Brookdown Crossing.

Beside the bridge, however, a far more unusual means of travel has gathered a curious audience. A young inventor proudly demonstrates what he calls his crane carriage—a stout wicker basket suspended from the long arm of a towering wooden crane. With much enthusiasm, he claims his remarkable contraption can carry passengers safely over rooftops and streets alike, setting them down at several landings across the town. For the modest fee of 1 Gold Piece, he offers passage to any of his regular destinations: Market Street, The Fitch or as far out as Pilgrims Way.
