You hand over a single Gold Piece, and the girl disappears into the opening with surprising agility. Following her below, you soon discover that her "tunnels" are in fact the town's ancient sewers. Narrow walkways wind beside sluggish channels of foul-smelling water, while damp stone walls glisten in the wavering light of her lantern. The air is thick with unpleasant odours, and more than once you are forced to duck beneath low arches or pick your way around puddles you would rather not examine too closely.

Despite the maze of passages, your young guide never hesitates. She darts confidently through twisting junctions and forgotten culverts until, after what feels like only a few minutes, daylight appears ahead. Climbing a short ladder, you emerge into the fresh air at Market Street, with a desperate urge to wash your hands.
