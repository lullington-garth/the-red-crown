You enter the town via Silk Street, passing beneath its soot-darkened archway and into a quieter quarter far removed from the noise of the market squares. Here the streets are narrow and uneven, lined with squat, clean but shabby houses.

You ride past the old Leyline Waystation where faded charms and coloured ribbons flutter from iron poles in the breeze. The air smells faintly of rain, chimney smoke and earth.

Further along the street you come upon a small, well-kept shop called Moonthread Runes. Unlike the buildings around it, the shopfront is freshly painted and carefully tended. Smooth white stones hang from silver wire above the doorway, each carved with glowing rune marks that pulse faintly in the dimly lit street.

A brass lantern swings gently above the door and warm golden light spills invitingly from the windows.
