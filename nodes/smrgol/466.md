The dragon regards you silently for several moments before speaking.

"You are either a brave one, or foolish," he chuckles then winces. His voice is deep, measured and calm, but it is obviously costing him talk. That doesn't stop him though. It seem that he is craving company, perhaps as a distraction from the pain he is evidently in.

He is surprising good company. Between wheezing rasping coughs he regales you with tales of lost kingdoms, vanished forests and ancient battles as though they happened only yesterday.

It is the mention of vanished forests that leads you to mention Antiquity.

"Every place of concentrated core magic has one," says the dragon. "They are rarely alone though. Where you find an Antiquity Tree you will also find a Silver Oak. You can tell them apart buy their glow. One blue, one green, although I'll be blessed if this old soldier can remember which way round it is." he chuckles and coughs again. He is getting weaker.

His eyes droop and a faint snort of smoke escapes his nostrils.
