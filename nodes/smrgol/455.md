A deafening roar echoes across the sky. Dragons. You dive for the cover of the undergrowth. 

At first you think they are travelling as a pack, unusual dragon behaviour, but as you watch it becomes clear that it is some kind if battle, no worse, six dragons circle a seventh, striking at it with tooth, claw and fire.

The lone dragon is enormous. Even from this distance you can make out the dark brown and scorched black scales of its back, and the deep orange glow of its underbelly. It fights with remarkable discipline, turning each attack aside with skill likely born of centuries of battle.

But it is not enough. Outnumbered and obviously exhausted, the ancient dragon finally falters. One of the attackers gets its jaw round the bigger dragon's neck while another shreds its wing. When they finally release the dragon the great beast plummets from the sky, fire pluming from its open mouth.

It crashes into the forest nearby like a comet. Earth, rock, trees, all the debris of a smashed woodland, are hurled into the air with explosive force, screaming through the air adding to the deafening roar of the shattered earth.
