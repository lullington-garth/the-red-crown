You open the door onto a long, narrow chamber lined with lecterns and measuring rods. Along both walls stand tall frames of brass and iron, each etched with runes and fine calibration marks. Slender rods, papers and scroll tubes are piled high upon layered shelves.

Scrolls hover in the air between the lecterns, some zipping through the room, others slowly revolving, their parchments glowing faintly with complicated rune-work.

At the far end of the chamber stands a tall desk of dark wood. Above it hangs a suspended frame of crystal lenses and rotating rings, slowly adjusting their angles with a soft whisper of motion. Every few moments a lens flashes briefly, casting a web of light across the room before dimming again.

A thin grey-robed man stands at the desk, his back to you, carefully marking symbols onto a slate with a piece of chalk.

He pauses, studies his work and makes a small correction.
