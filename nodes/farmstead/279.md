‘On your head be it,’ she says. ‘Go dig it up, Fergus.’ Her farmhand darts off with a wave of her hand.

You wait for some time before the fellow finally returns, sweating and filthy from digging. He carries a mud-stained sack which he hands to the farmer.

She reaches inside and withdraws a plain wooden box bound with tarnished iron bands. The wood is dark as charcoal and cold enough to sting your fingers.

Even before touching it, you can feel faint magic pulsing within.
