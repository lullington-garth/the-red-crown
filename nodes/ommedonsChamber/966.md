At the end of the passage stands a pair of immense doors, taller than any tower you have yet seen in the fortress. They are forged from a dark, cold metal and seem to drink in the light. Their surfaces are engraved with scenes of conquest, ruin and devastation. Everywhere you look the doors depict dominion; cities crumbling beneath a giant clawed hand, kings kneel before a robed mage, dragons coiled around flaming and broken kingdoms.

As you step closer, you feel a pressure exuding from them. A thickening of the air, like the weight of a gathering storm.

You are sure the engravings are moving, but when you look directly at them they are still.

Despite their size, there is no lock. No handle. No visible means of opening the doors at all. Instead, engraved at their centre is a gnarled and fearsome oak. Upon its trunk is a shallow depression—smooth, oval, and unmistakably shaped.
