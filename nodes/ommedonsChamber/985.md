You raise a shield in one hand and your sword in the other as you approach the barrier.

You swing at the closest shard and, as you expected, it shatters easily, sending thousands of smaller shards whirling in all directions. But some of those fragments rip your skin as they fly past.

The pain makes you jolt backwards into a passing shard taller than you. It too shatters, spraying you with hundreds of tiny, razor-sharp splinters.

You raise your shield against them, but it can only block so much and, with it held high, you miss another incoming shard. It explodes over you, thousands of spinning fragments tearing at your skin.

You gasp and stagger into another fragile sheet of whirling ice.

And so it continues.

Roll two dice. This is the number of shards you shatter as you blunder your way through the barrier. Each shard you shatter costs you 1 STAMINA point.

If you survive, you crash out of the far side of the wall of protection, severely weakened. 

LOSE 2 SKILL points.

The spinning shards fall to the ground, melting away. Within seconds, the only evidence of their short-lived existence is the wounds you carry with you.
