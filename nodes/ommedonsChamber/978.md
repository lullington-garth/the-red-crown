Peter meets Ommadon's eyes. He is dressed in a flowing suit of soft velvet. He raises a hand — and magic floods the air, coalescing around him. You can feel Peter drawing upon the power within you and, from the look of surprise on Ommadon's face, from every source available to him. You. Ommadon. Antiquity. The chamber itself.

The runes carved into the walls flare with blinding light. The great tree etched in blue swells, its roots tightening as if bracing for something immense. Peter raises his arm and begins a low, rhythmic chant, weaving spell upon spell with impossible precision — wards layered within counter-wards, bindings wrapped around bindings, magic folding in upon itself like a collapsing star.

Ommadon draws deeply, calling upon darkness, hate, bitterness, fear and pain. Every ounce of dark magic he commands pours into the chamber. The stone groans as power meets power, the two immense forces grinding against one another in a howling maelstrom.

Ommadon's eyes widen. His power, though vast, is not enough. The more roiling, hate-filled magic he hurls at Peter, the stronger Peter becomes. Each attack only feeds him. Every spell becomes fuel for the fire he is building.

Ommadon is no longer a rival, he is a source of power. He falls to his knees. His form begins to wither and fade until, with a final explosive burst of red light, he is gone.

His crown clatters to the floor as In a burst of blinding light Peter is gone too,

You stand alone in the enveloping silence of the mighty chamber.

You bend down and lift the crown, noticing at once the vast, sickening absence. The magic is no longer there. It is not only the lifeless crown in your hands that has been stripped of magic. It is you as well. The chamber. Antiquity itself.

The power that won this victory demanded fuel beyond measure. Magic has been spent. Completely consumed.

With growing horror, you watch the blue glow fade from the walls. The roots slacken. The great tree dims, its brilliance draining away until only a dull, lifeless husk remains.

You hold the crown, but without magic it is little more than a symbol — beautiful, ancient and powerless.

The magical realm will endure for a time, but without living magic it cannot last.

You have claimed the crown, but at the cost of the world you came to protect.

Magic alone was never the answer.
