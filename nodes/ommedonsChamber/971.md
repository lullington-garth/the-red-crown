Forgotten words flare in your mind and you gasp them out:

"Truth is what remains when certainty subsides."

The effect is immediate. The cloud releases its grip on you and you drag in air. Lightning flickers around you and fades. The smoke thins, peeling back in slow spiralling waves as the oppressive pressure lifts from your chest.

As your vision clears, you see Ommadon's chamber for the first time.

The chamber is vast, circular and impossibly tall, the ceiling lost in thunderous rolling cloud through which red lightning streaks silently. In the centre of the room is a clear, still pool, surrounded by a lattice of knotted blue veins. Blue light bleeds faintly from the veins, pulsing like an ancient heartbeat. The veins fan out from the pool to cover one entire wall of the throne room, forming the shape of an ancient oak—Antiquity.

The opposite wall is dominated by a colossal window of fractured crystal through which the roiling red sky of Ommadon's dominion can be seen.

At the far end of the vast hall, raised upon a dais of black stone, sits Ommadon on a throne of dark red crystal. The crystal appears to have grown unnaturally, forced into a jagged geometry roughly resembling Ommadon's crown, the source of his power. He surveys you with cold indifference.

You are still taking in the majesty of the chamber when the fading cloud starts to coalesce, taking form.

From the receding cloud emerge Ommadon's sentinels—constructs of will and malice. Tall, distorted figures, half-formed from smoke and crackling energy, their outlines constantly shifting, blades dripping darkness held in insubstantial, formless hands.

They form a ring around you.
