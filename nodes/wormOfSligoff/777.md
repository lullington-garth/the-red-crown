Drawing Sir Orrin's Sword, you charge at the Worm. It moves with a grace and speed that belies its size, lunging towards you, its mouth agape.

You swing the enchanted blade. It slices through the creature's flesh, cutting with almost no resistance. The beast howls with a sickening screech as you swing again and slice it cleanly in half. Two pieces of worm crash to earth, sending up sprays of acid slime as they collide with the pool.

You press your advantage, the magical sword flashing through the air as great chunks of the beast tumble into the acid, writhing and thrashing as they sink beneath the bubbling slime.

You are wiping the acidic slime from your sword, the battle won, when to your horror tiny heads begin to sprout from the torn flesh of the worm. Tails form at the other end of the cut pieces, writhing and squirming until each has become a living worm in its own right.

Only now do you realise that you could cut it into a hundred pieces and each part would live to spread the slime.

The pool seethes with the newly formed worms, all turning towards you with hungry intent.

Roll one die to determine the number of worm-spawn that crawl from the slime to attack.
