The knight stands rigid beside an overturned chair, one hand resting upon the hilt of his sword, the other upon his chest. His polished armour gleams in the firelight bright against the grime of the tavern around him.

‘I shall not permit such insults against the lady Melisande!’ he declares.

The five men seated around the table burst into laughter. Thick-necked and hard-faced, they look like the worst kind of thugs. Tankards slosh as they jeer and imitate the knight’s noble accent.

‘Five duels?’ one snarls. ‘You offering to die five times over?’

The men roar with laughter, but their leader, a man so large you wonder if he is part troll, has had enough. He stands, throwing his tankard to the saw-dusted floor. The others follow at once, benches screeching backwards, the table toppling over as they rise.

A cheer goes up from the nearby tables as the men close in around the lone knight.
