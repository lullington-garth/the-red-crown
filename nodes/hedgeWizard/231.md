Recognising the name you interrupt the quarrel. The trader looks mutinous and the wizard surprised. You hand the latter the box you picked up at the glassblowers. He looks a little confused, but as he opens the box, his face lights up. He removes several polished stone orbs which he immediately hands to the trader.

‘Chill stones,’ he says. ‘I had them made for you, for your milk. Even the hottest summer day will not spoil it now.’

The trader’s anger melts away at once. He looks almost teary. He grabs the wizard in a bear hug.

‘Shabanaka you big lug. Thank you.’ He smiles broadly. ‘And for you,’ he says nodding at you, ‘for stopping me killing my best mate, some of the best milk in Brookdown Crossing.’

The milk is good indeed.
