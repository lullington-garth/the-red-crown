Up close, the scale of the carcass becomes overwhelming. The desert wind moans through a tangled ruin of bones, too vast and too distorted to easily identify what manner of creature it once was. Whatever it was, it must have been enormous in life—its ribs like broken arches, its skull split and half-buried in sand, its limbs scattered as though torn apart by something stronger still. Time and heat have bleached it to a pale, unnatural white beneath the red sky.
