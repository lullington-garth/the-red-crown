The stench of scorched scale and blood hangs in the air behind you as you leave the broken body of Bryagh where it fell.

Ahead, Ommadon's fortress rises sheer and black from the rock face. Set into the harsh stone are two enormous doors. They appear to be fashioned from a dark, ancient wood, smoothed and worn by age so that they now have the sheen of polished stone.

Carved across the sealed doors is a vast serpent, its body coiled and looping from threshold to lintel. It lies upon a bed of hundreds of smaller snakes which entwine its coils, each biting its own tail, their eyes picked out in dull green stone.

You can see no handle, lock or hinges. As you would expect, it appears to be a barrier of ancient magic.

As you peer up at the door, you cannot shake the feeling that perhaps the door is staring back at you.
