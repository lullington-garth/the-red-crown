You descend the narrow stairs, whispering a little magic to light your way. It is a long way down and as you reach the bottom of the stairs the flickering glow reveals a small underground chamber carved directly from the rock itself. Symbols are scratched and chalked upon the walls and low ceiling, though their use and meaning are not obvious to you.

It’s an odd room. A small desk stands against one wall, though even with a lantern there would be far too little light here for study. Scattered bottles and cracked vials litter the floor, and in the gloom you make out a large symbol chalked upon the rough stone ground. Nearby on the wall, the symbols give way to a crudely constructed board from which hangs a dusty parchment, covered in jagged, frantic lines. One thick black line cuts wildly from one side of the page to the other as if this inking was somehow important to the madness that produced it.
