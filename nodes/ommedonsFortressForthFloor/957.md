You place the acorn into the hollow.

The stone beneath it cracks and the ground roars. The silver-blue lines surge forward, enveloping the acorn in a binding, crushing grip. The etchings on the walls flare blindingly bright, then settle into a steady, powerful pulsing rhythm echoing through the chamber like a mighty beating heart.

The pool churns and clears.

Peter stands opposite a an older man, a pawnbroker by the looks of the surroundings. A board game lies between them, its pieces arranged for combat.

Peter reaches out and selects a single piece—a knight in heavy armour. The pawnbroker raises an eyebrow, but Peter moves the piece with purpose, sweeping others aside and clearing a path through the enemy force.

The vision ends.

The silver-blue threads binding the acorn retreat and the chamber falls silent.
