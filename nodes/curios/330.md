You head down Old Curiosity Lane until the leaning houses and rain-blackened brickwork give way to a strange little shop squeezed between two taller buildings. Its crooked sign creaks gently overhead, the faded gold lettering reading Curios. The building itself appears ancient, with warped beams bulging beneath cracked plaster walls and upper windows that jut out over the street as though listening for secrets below.

In the dusty display window stand objects that seem gathered from dreams rather than workshops: a clock with no hands ticking softly to itself, a silver fish suspended in a jar of dark liquid, and a porcelain mask whose painted eyes appear to follow you as you move. Somewhere inside the shop you hear the faint chime of metal striking metal.
