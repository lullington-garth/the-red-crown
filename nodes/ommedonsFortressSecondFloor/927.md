The moment you reach for the acorn, the shimmering light bleeds through dark red.

The tree shudders, heaves, and tears itself apart. The torn sections form limbs and a crude, gnarled head with a single silver acorn eye. It rises before you, four mismatched arms ending in tangled twig-like hands protruding from its thick, bulbous trunk. It stands tall, taller than the tree it tore itself from, balanced upon three root-twisted legs. It glares down at you with its single acorn eye.

The tree's voice, when it speaks, is layered, as if the words were uttered by a crowd reciting verse rather than a single mouth ripped from heavy bark:

"I AM THE ANSWER BEFORE YOU NOW."

You stand momentarily bewildered.
