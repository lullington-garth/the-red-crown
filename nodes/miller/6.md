You raise your voice above the grind of timber and water. "Take care. Be careful with your machines I say."

The mill door slams open. Three scrawny men emerge, sleeves rolled, faces sharp with irritation. One squints across the river, then barks a laugh. “What’s this? A hedge-wizard lost his way?” Another stoops, snatching a stone. “Be gone, you relic!”

he hurls the rock your way, it skips wide a second in close sucession.
