The flame flares bright—blue-green, alive—then gutters mid-flight, sputtering into ash before it can reach the wheel. The river swallows the last of its light.
For a heartbeat, silence. Then laughter.
“Look at that!” one of the men jeers. “All smoke and no fire!” His mate, laughing, snatches up a stone and hurls it hard at you. It marks the beginning of another volley.
Their voices rise, sharp and cruel, driving you back from the bank. Your jaw tightens. There is nothing more to be done here. You turn away, leaving the river—and their laughter ringing behind you.
