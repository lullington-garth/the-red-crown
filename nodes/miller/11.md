The fireball arcs across the river in a streak of blue-green fury and strikes the waterwheel dead-centre. Wood screams and iron shrieks as the magical fire rips into it. The wheel shatters in a burst of steam and splintered beams, the current surging free as the crippled wheel topples from its spindle.

The men drop their stones, falling to their knees before scrambling backward, white-faced. Then they run—stumbling and slipping in their haste to flee the unnatural fire and its caster.

As the dust settles you feel regret at your actions. This will mend nothing. The divide between man and magic here is already strained to breaking, and you have only driven the wedge deeper.

And yet… You feel it still—the echo of the spell thrumming through your bones. Strong. Clean. Invigorating. That’s the first time it has answered your call and that gives you hope.
