After presenting your token, you are eventually led to a quieter corner of the Exchange where a weary-looking witch sits hunched behind a velvet-covered table. Candles gutter around her beneath floating runes of pale blue light.

She is arguing forcibly with a huge knight who is carrying enough weapons to arm a crusade.

“One… item… per… visit!” she snaps, stabbing a finger towards a large wooden sign bearing the same message. “If you want another enchantment, join the back of the queue like everyone else!”

The knight grumbles loudly but eventually slumps off muttering curses.

The witch rubs her temples the looks up at you.

“One object enchanted per visit. Ten Gold Pieces,” she says in a tired tone. “Effects are not guaranteed and vary. Most enchantments will improve item stats, not all though. If your item already has stats, the new stats will be in addition to them. If while enchanting a new special ability it added to your item, any old ability will be removed. Stats are not special abilities. Some enchantments may change the purpose of your item. No refunds.” She says all this very fast. You get the impression that she says this a lot.
