The stall owner glances up from his scales as you mention curse removal. His pale fingers continue sorting black crystals while he speaks in a dry, rasping voice.

“Most curses are simple things,” he mutters. “Bad bargains. Angry witches. Haunted jewellery. Remove the tether, cleanse the soul, restore the balance.”

From beneath the counter he produces a silver knife, several black candles and a clay bowl filled with dark water that reflects no light.

“For 5 Gold Pieces I can strip a curse clean from flesh or spirit. Painful, of course, but survivable.”
He pauses before reaching beneath the counter once more and placing a small wooden case before you. Strange symbols have been burned into the lid.

“Or perhaps you prefer to handle such matters yourself? Starter curse-breaker scrolls. Four Gold Pieces each. Tug the Black Thread and Witch Knot Sundered. Very popular among adventurers who expect poor decisions.”
