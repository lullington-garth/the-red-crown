You step into the immense corridor behind the gatekeeper.

Almost immediately, a low tremor passes beneath your feet.

The floor shivers, sending small showers of dust drifting from the distant ceiling. The silver flames dancing within the great braziers flicker for a moment before settling once more, while the colourful light spilling through the stained-glass windows dims as some unseen shadow passed outside.

The disturbance fades almost as quickly as it began.

The gatekeeper neither slows nor acknowledges it and you have to hurry to catch him up. You are surprised at how quickly you catch him.

Distance seems to be fluid within the passage. Each step you take seems to carry you much farther than it should. The vast stained-glass windows drift past more quickly than seems possible, and the great doorway ahead approaches far quicker than your stride would dictate.

You glance at the silent gatekeeper walking ahead wondering if perhaps passages such as these explain how he could be at both the beginning and the end of your journey through this realm.

Soon you stand before the great arched doorway. A mighty oak has been carved into its surface, every branch and root rendered in astonishing detail.

The gatekeeper turns towards you. "Touch the acorn to the door."

You place the silver acorn against the carved tree.

Silver light races through the carving and the great doors silently swing open.
