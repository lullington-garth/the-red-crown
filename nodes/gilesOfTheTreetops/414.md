Further along the road, you come upon an ancient stone marker standing beside the path. It is both taller and broader than you, its weathered surface worn by countless years. Yet despite its age, the intricate carvings remain remarkably clear.

The stone depicts two great oak trees sharing a single oversized acorn. At the centre of the acorn is a smooth, circular depression, as though it were carved to receive a missing object. Upon the broad trunk of each tree is another hollow, shaped unmistakably like a human hand: one left, one right.
