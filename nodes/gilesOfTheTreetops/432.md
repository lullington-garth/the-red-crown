Arrows whistle somewhere above you.

At first you think you are dreaming. The flowers woven into the net have filled your head with a heavy fog and the world seems distant and unreal. Through blurred eyes you see shapes moving amongst the trees and hear shouts from somewhere overhead.

Then comes another arrow.

The shaft flashes through the clearing and pins one of the wood elves to a nearby trunk. A second follows almost immediately, catching another through his tunic and sending him sprawling against a tree.

More arrows streak from the trees with astonishing accuracy, striking belts, straps and collars and pinning the startled elves helplessly in place. Those hidden in the canopy scramble for cover while others stare in disbelief at their trapped companions.

Through your fading vision you see a slender figure emerge from the forest. Yes, you think, you must be dreaming.

The woman kneels beside you and cuts through the enchanted net with a hunting knife.

"Easy now," she says. "The flowers will pass soon enough."

She helps you to your feet before turning her attention towards the pinned leader.

"Giles," she says. "This one is a friend."

She pulls the arrow free and the elf drops awkwardly to the ground, looking utterly mortified.

"I am deeply sorry," he says, addressing you. "I mistook you for one of Ommadon's dark wizards. We have seen many of their kind travelling this road of late. I am Giles of the Treetops, and these are my men."

He gestures awkwardly towards the other elves still dangling from the surrounding trees.

As your head gradually clears, you recognise the woman as Danielle of the Woods. The lady you met at the Enchanter's Exchange. You thank her for intervening and  help her free the remaining prisoners while other elves descend from the canopy. You quickly discover that there are far more of them hidden in the branches than you first imagined and, despite their unfortunate introduction, they prove to be a cheerful and good-natured company.

They listen with great interest as you explain your quest.

"You'll be wanting Dragon Rose," says Giles when you speak of entering Ommadon's Red Keep. "Only thing likely to get you past that brute of a dragon, Bryagh. Rare flower, mind you. Grows deep in abandoned mines where few folk dare to go. Powerful stuff. Any dragon that catches its scent will soon be asleep. Bryagh's a monster, but even he'll struggle against it."

He then hands you a thorned club about the length of a short sword.

"Worm Slayer," he says reverently. "Doesn't look like much, but against worms and crawlers—and I hear Ommadon keeps some particularly nasty ones—it is devastating."
