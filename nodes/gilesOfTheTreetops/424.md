You tell Danielle of the ambush and the warriors who attacked you. You explain how you fought for your life and how the world is a better place without low life brigands roaming the forest.

It is as you describe the falling net and the oaf armed with a thorned club that Danielle's expression darkens.

"You fool," she says, drawing her sword. "That was Giles of the Treetops. One of the last true friends of these woods and a dear friend of mine."

She step towards you, a dangerous anger burning in her eyes.
