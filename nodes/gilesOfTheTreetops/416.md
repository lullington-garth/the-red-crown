Arrows whistle through the clearing.

At first you think that you are under attack, but the arrows are not aimed at you. Instead, the shafts streak across the road and strike the wood elves with astonishing precision, pinning tunics, belts and collars to the trees behind them.

You stare in amazement. The elves appear equally stunned as they dangle helplessly from the trunks. Whoever loosed these arrows possesses frightening skill.

You turn to thank your unseen rescuer and Danielle of the Woods steps calmly from the forest.

"Giles," she says sternly as she approaches the pinned leader. "This one is a friend."

She pulls the arrow free and the elf drops awkwardly to the ground, looking utterly mortified.

"I am deeply sorry," he says, addressing you. "I mistook you for one of Ommadon's dark wizards. We have seen many of their kind travelling this road of late. I am Giles of the Treetops, and these are my men."

He gestures awkwardly towards the other elves still hanging from the surrounding trees.

You assure him that no harm has been done. As you help free his companions and the remaining elves descend from the canopy, you discover that there are many more of them hidden above, and that despite their rough introduction they are a cheerful and good-natured company.

They listen eagerly as you explain your quest.

"You'll be wanting Dragon Rose," says Giles when you mention your need to enter Ommadon's Red Keep. "Only thing likely to get you past that brute of a dragon, Bryagh. Rare flower, mind you. Grows deep in abandoned mines where few folk dare to go. Powerful stuff. Any dragon that catches its scent will soon be asleep. Bryagh's a  onster, but even he'll struggle against it."

He then hands you a thorned club about the length of a short sword.

"Worm Slayer," he says reverently. "Doesn't look like much, but against worms and crawlers—and I hear Ommadon keeps some particularly nasty ones—it is devastating."
