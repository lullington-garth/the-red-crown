You continue your search for the main road, weaving your way between ancient trees and thick banks of fern. Before long, you notice a thin thread of smoke rising through the branches ahead.

Proceeding cautiously, you move closer until you can make out a small woodland hut nestled amongst the trees. Nearby stands a slender red-haired woman dressed in buff hide trousers, a green tunic and a leather waistcoat.

She kneels beside a wounded deer, carefully wrapping one of its legs with strips of cloth. A longbow rests against a nearby stump, within easy reach.

When she finishes tending the animal, she rises and gently sends it back into the forest. The deer bounds away between the trees while the woman watches until it disappears from sight.

The woman then turns, reaches calmly for her bow and before you know what is happening, an arrow slams into the tree beside your head.

"Come out or be gone," she calls. "The woods have already told me you are there."

A second arrow whistles through the air, tearing through the shoulder of your robe. She already has another shaft resting against the bowstring.
