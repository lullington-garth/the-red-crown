Gormley Keep rises before you like the face of a sheer, impassable mountain. Its enormous gates are dwarfed by the vast wall of grey stone that marks the edge of Ommadon's realm. The gate is not in use; if you wish to enter the realm, you must climb the stone steps to the top of the wall and face the Ogre of Gormley Keep.

You knew even before you checked your maps that Gormley Keep is a vast structure built to guard the only pass through a black, hostile mountain range which runs for days in both directions. There is no avoiding it, and Ommadon has ensured that there is no means of passing it, or the ogre, by magical means. And so, forcing back the sense of dread the towering wall fills you with, and trying not to think about what awaits you above, you find the steps and start the perilous climb up the face of the keep.

It takes you two exhausting and arduous days to reach the small iron gate that marks the top of the rise. Beyond the gate is a small, low tunnel, and beyond that, the ogre.

You camp in the freezing tunnel for the night in the hope of gathering your strength before facing the ogre, but the bitter howling wind and the damp, rough-hewn rock of the tunnel put pay to any real rest.
