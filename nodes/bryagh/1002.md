It is as you gather your make to step past the enormous body of the fallen dragon, that you notice your horse. It lays slashed and panting a victim of the battle, its life force spilling out onto the scorched red sand. You kneel beside the beast, stroking its mane, doing your best to comfort it in its final moments. It brings you great sadness as the horse slips into death, its ragged breathing now still. It also presents you with a problem. Your horse was carrying a lot of your equipment. You must decide what items are essential to carry going forwards.
