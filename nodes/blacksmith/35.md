You head deeper into town along Bellfounders Way, where the sound of industry grows louder and more constant. Hammer strikes ring out in steady rhythm, echoing between tall timbered buildings and soot-darkened stone. The air carries the smell of hot metal and coal smoke, drifting through shafts of afternoon sun.

Ahead, a blacksmith works outside his open-fronted forge, sleeves rolled up and face streaked with grime and sweat. Behind him, the workshop is crowded with racks of tools, weapons and half-finished armour, all catching the firelight from within. Sparks drift out onto the street with each strike of his hammer. The road here is busier too—porters, guards and merchants passing by without stopping for long.

The blacksmith pauses as you approach, resting his hammer on a cooling anvil.
