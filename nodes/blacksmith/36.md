The blacksmith seems pleased. “Simple enough game,” he says. “I play odds, you play evens. We both roll three dice and discard one. If we both roll evens, you win. If we both roll odds, I win. Doesn’t matter about the score then—but if you roll even and I roll odd, highest score takes it. If I roll even and you roll odd, we’ll call that a draw.” He chuckles, tapping the edge of his anvil. “Let’s make it interesting, shall we? Three gold pieces per round to the winner. Not going to force you to play. We’ll stop whenever you’ve had enough… or can’t afford the wager,” he laughs again.

With a grin, he scatters a handful of coins across his anvil and produces a worn leather bag of dice, letting them rattle in his palm as he waits for your answer.

You can play as many rounds as you like while you have the money to do so.
