The landing gives way beneath you with a sickening scream of tearing timber. You plunge to the floor below, masonry, beams, timber and dust sucked into the opening with you. You hit the ground hard, the breath driven from your lungs as shattered staircase crashes around you. You land heavily on your backpack. It takes the brunt of the blow, and you are lucky to have taken no damage yourself, but as you make to stand after the fall something tells you that your possessions may not have fared so well.

You dust yourself off, ears ringing thinking that you probably need to check your possessions for damage.
